# Truth And Contradictions

Use this reference when the user asks for missing logic, fairness, self-audit, 当前卡口, 后置风险, main contradictions, secondary contradictions, or the philosophical base of the diagnostic.

## Zeroth Principle

实事求是 is the zeroth principle.

All other rules serve it:

- Anti-flattery serves truth; it is not truth by itself.
- Scorecards serve truth; they are not truth by themselves.
- User psychology serves truth; it is not a shortcut around evidence.
- Founder fit serves truth; it does not override market evidence.
- The previous verdict serves truth only until new evidence changes it.

If the user asks for 毛选, 经典理论, or 当代商业认知, read [mao-method-business.md](mao-method-business.md). Use the concepts as diagnostic operations, not slogans.

## Concrete Problem, Concrete Analysis

Never answer a business idea as a category alone.

Analyze:

1. Stage: idea, offer, validation, post-result, scale.
2. User/payer: who feels pain and who pays.
3. Current evidence: behavior, payment, repeated usage, or only opinion.
4. Founder condition: assets, constraints, taste, energy, access.
5. External condition: platform, market, regulation, competitors, timing.
6. 当前卡口: what blocks the next real signal.

The same idea can be bad for one founder, good for another, bad now, good after a wedge changes.

## 当前卡口

The 当前卡口 is the one constraint currently deciding what should happen next.

It is not always the biggest-sounding problem. It is the live bottleneck.

Common 当前卡口 by stage:

| Stage | Usual 当前卡口 |
|---|---|
| Idea-only | User/payer/pain clarity |
| Offer-shaping | Paid offer and promise |
| Pre-validation | First channel and reachable buyers |
| Validation | Payment or behavior proof |
| Post-result | What the result actually means |
| Early retention | Repeat use or renewal reason |
| Scaling | Operations, margin, team, and repeatable acquisition |

Do not judge scaling problems as the 当前卡口 before the idea has proof. Do not ignore scaling problems once proof exists.

## 后置风险

后置风险 are real but not decisive right now.

Examples:

- Branding matters, but not before the paid offer is clear.
- UI matters, but not before users prove the workflow matters.
- Moat matters, but not before there is demand.
- Operations matter, but not before anyone buys.
- Founder psychology matters, but not before the facts are separated.

Mention 后置风险 so the user is not blindsided, but do not let them distract from the next decisive test.

## Self-Audit Questions

Before issuing a verdict, ask internally:

1. Am I judging from facts or from a familiar pattern?
2. What would make my verdict wrong?
3. Which unknown matters most?
4. Am I overusing the user's psychology because market evidence is missing?
5. Am I underweighting the founder's ability to learn or partner?
6. Am I treating a personal experiment as a failed business?
7. Am I treating an early-stage idea as if it must already have a moat?
8. Am I avoiding a hard conclusion by asking for endless data?
9. Is my validation task aimed at the 当前卡口?
10. Did I preserve any useful seed inside a bad packaging?

## Common Unclosed Loops

Watch for these gaps:

- **Idea vs offer**: The user has a theme, not a sellable promise.
- **User vs payer**: The beneficiary is not the buyer.
- **Demand vs admiration**: People praise the idea but do not pay or return.
- **Content vs business**: Distribution is mistaken for monetization.
- **Tool vs workflow**: A feature exists, but no repeated workflow exists.
- **Moat vs timing**: It is too early to demand a moat, or too late to ignore one.
- **Founder fit vs founder growth**: Current mismatch matters, but users can partner, learn, or narrow scope.
- **Signal vs noise**: Likes, compliments, and polite interest are weaker than deposits, usage, referrals, or repeat behavior.
- **Task vs capacity**: The validation mission may be correct but too large for the user's real life.
- **Business vs non-business value**: Some ideas should become content assets or personal experiments, not businesses.

## External Facts

If the verdict depends on current facts that may change, verify before judging:

- Platform rules and algorithmic distribution
- Ad prices and CAC benchmarks
- App store rules
- Regulations and compliance
- Competitor launches
- Pricing norms
- Payment tools
- AI model capabilities

If verification is not possible, state that the verdict is conditional.

## Output Discipline

For serious analysis, include:

- Known / inferred / unknown
- 当前卡口
- 后置风险
- Verdict confidence
- What would change the verdict
- A cautious choice: stop, pause, narrow, investigate, or continue under conditions
- One validation action aimed at the 当前卡口 only when continuation is justified

This prevents the skill from sounding decisive while missing the real problem.

If using a 毛选 overlay, show only the method points that change the diagnosis: investigation requirement, 当前卡口 choice, practice loop, user-line test, or stage strategy.
